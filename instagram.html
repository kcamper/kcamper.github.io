Targeted and personalized Instagram ads

Knowing how Instagram can give you targeted ads based on not only your location, interests, and likes, but also based on data from third-parties, I decided to look further into how personalized my ads were. I noticed that Instagram would sometimes give me ads based on products that I looked at outside of the app, but I wasn't sure how the rest of my ads differed from others'.

I recorded myself scrolling through my feed and asked one of my friends to do the same.

I tend to see a lot of clothing and fashion ads, along with ads for social media apps and subscription box services. Other than following people I know, I only follow a couple fashion and clothing brands/accounts, so the ads I see make sense. For comparison, the following video is my friend's screen recording of him using Instagram.

He also follows people that he knows, several clothing brands, and a few celebrities and politicians. We live nearby and are the same age, and he sometimes sends me links to products that I end up seeing as ads on Instagram and Facebook, so I wasn't sure how much our ads would differ. Despite following clothing brands and often looking at coffee products, most of his ads were for Microsoft and various universities and grad schools.

We often only see our own ads, so it can be hard to tell how personal and targeted our ads really are, so if you use Instagram, feel free to take a look at what type of ads you get and compare them with what I found.
